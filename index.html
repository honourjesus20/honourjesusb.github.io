About

I am an independent researcher working on building language and speech models for African languages and accents in education.

My research develops ASR and TTS systems for low-resource languages to improve educational accessibility and equity in multilingual African contexts. I'm currently investigating several interconnected approaches: domain-adapted fine-tuning strategies for speech models in code-switched environments, data augmentation techniques for low-resource language pairs, and efficient inference methods for deploying ASR-TTS systems in resource-constrained settings. My broader research vision centres on demonstrating that linguistic equity and technical performance are not opposing forces — thoughtful system design can bridge language barriers while maintaining the quality standards required for high-stakes educational applications.

Beyond research, I am passionate about mentoring aspiring data professionals and building open science ML communities. You’ll find me actively involved with ML Collective, Cohere Labs, and other collaborative initiatives. I firmly believe in democratizing access to knowledge, fostering collaborative ecosystems, and championing the ethos of shared discovery.

When I’m not doing research, I enjoy playing chess, cooking, fashion illustration, reading books, and listening to podcasts.

Research / Projects

ASR & TTS for Low‑Resource African Languages

Developing automatic speech recognition and text-to-speech pipelines adapted for code‑switched and low-resource settings. Methods include domain-adapted fine-tuning, targeted data augmentation, and efficient inference strategies for edge deployment in educational contexts.

Keywords: ASR, TTS, low-resource NLP, code-switching, model compression, education tech.

Domain‑Adapted Fine‑Tuning for Speech Models

Investigating fine-tuning strategies and data selection to adapt large pretrained speech models to regional accents and multilingual classrooms.
